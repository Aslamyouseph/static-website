ALAPPUZHA



Alappuzha, often referred to as the "Venice of the East," is a charming town nestled in the picturesque backwaters of Kerala, South India. Renowned for its labyrinthine network of canals, tranquil lagoons, and vibrant houseboat cruises, Alappuzha offers visitors a unique and unforgettable experience. The town is also famous for its annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, where majestic snake boats race along the Punnamada Lake, drawing crowds from far and wide. Beyond its waterways, Alappuzha boasts lush greenery, swaying coconut palms, and idyllic paddy fields, providing a serene backdrop for leisurely walks and nature appreciation. With its rich cultural heritage, warm hospitality, and breathtaking scenery, Alappuzha captivates the hearts of travelers seeking an authentic Kerala experience.
